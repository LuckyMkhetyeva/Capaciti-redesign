About CAPACITI

CAPACITI is a division of the Technology Innovation Agency, dedicated to building South Africa's digital economy by developing world-class tech talent and fostering innovation across communities.

Our Mission

To accelerate careers in technology by providing comprehensive training, mentorship, and employment opportunities that bridge the skills gap in South Africa's digital economy.

Our Vision

To be the leading catalyst for digital transformation in Africa, creating sustainable pathways for underrepresented communities to thrive in the technology sector.

History & Timeline

CAPACITI, part of the UVU Africa Group, was established in 2010 as a digital talent accelerator in South Africa, focusing on training and placing unemployed youth into tech careers. It is known for its work in developing digital skills and preparing individuals for the evolving digital economy.

  • 2010

    CAPACITI is founded as a digital career accelerator by the Cape Innovation and Technology Initiative (CiTi).

  • 2018

    CAPACITI scaled its operations to include Johannesburg, alongside Cape Town, to support youth and businesses.

  • 2020

    CAPACITI began a year-long program with phased approaches, including internship phases with companies like Centrax Systems.

  • 2021

    CAPACITI, in partnership with the City of Cape Town, launched a program focused on software development and programming languages.

  • 2024

    UVU Africa announced a new Advanced Technical Academy in Braamfontein, focusing on DevOps, Java, and Data Analytics, with intensive training and career-critical skills development.

  • 2025 (May)

    CAPACITI highlighted the critical role of tech talent in Africa's e-commerce success, emphasizing the growing tech skills gap.
